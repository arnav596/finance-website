What is Financial Literacy?

Financial literacy is the ability to understand and manage money effectively. It includes knowledge about income, expenses, saving, investing, budgeting, and financial decision-making.

A financially literate person can plan expenses, save for future goals, avoid unnecessary debt, and grow wealth responsibly.

Income

Income is the money you earn from various sources such as salary, business profits, freelancing, interest, dividends, or rental income.

Understanding income sources helps in better budgeting and planning. Diversifying income is important for financial stability.

Expenses

Expenses are the money spent on daily needs, wants, and obligations such as food, rent, education, transportation, and entertainment.

Tracking expenses helps control overspending and improves saving habits.

Assets

Assets are resources that generate income or increase in value over time. Examples include stocks, mutual funds, gold, property, and fixed deposits.

Building assets is essential for long-term financial growth.

Liabilities

Liabilities are financial obligations or debts such as loans, credit card dues, or EMIs.

Excessive liabilities reduce financial freedom and should be managed carefully.

Saving & Budgeting

Saving is setting aside a portion of income for future needs. Budgeting is planning income and expenses to ensure savings.

A proper budget helps achieve financial goals and avoid unnecessary debt.

Investing

Stocks

Stocks represent ownership in a company. They offer high returns but come with higher risk.

Mutual Funds

Mutual funds pool money from investors and invest in diversified assets. They are suitable for beginners through SIPs.

Gold

Gold is considered a safe investment and helps protect wealth during inflation and economic uncertainty.

Trading

Trading involves buying and selling financial instruments for short-term gains. It carries high risk and is not suitable for beginners.
